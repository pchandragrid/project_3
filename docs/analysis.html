Analysis & Results

Comprehensive evaluation of model performance, including beam search experiments and caption length analysis.

Performance Metrics

We primarily use the CIDEr (Consensus-based Image Description Evaluation) score to measure the quality of generated captions.

Why CIDEr?

Unlike BLEU or METEOR, CIDEr is specifically designed for image captioning. It rewards captions that capture the consensus of multiple human references, making it a robust metric for "human-likeness."

Goal: Improve CIDEr by >10% over baseline.

Experiment 1: Beam Search vs. Quality

We investigated the impact of Beam Size on caption quality. Beam search explores multiple potential sentence completions in parallel, rather than just picking the single most likely word at each step (Greedy Search).

Figure 1: CIDEr scores across different beam sizes for BLIP, ViT-GPT2, and GIT.

Key Findings

  • Improvement: Increasing beam size generally improves CIDEr scores, especially from 1 (Greedy) to 3.
  • Diminishing Returns: Beyond a beam size of 5, the improvement plateaus or even degrades slightly due to the model generating overly generic or repetitive phrases.
  • Recommendation: A beam size of 3-5 offers the best trade-off between quality and inference speed.

Experiment 2: Caption Length Analysis

We analyzed how model performance varies with the length of the ground-truth captions. Are models better at short, simple descriptions or long, complex ones?

Figure 2: CIDEr scores broken down by caption length category (Short, Medium, Long).

Key Findings

  • Short Captions: Models perform best on short, concise captions. The vocabulary is often simpler and the sentence structure more predictable.
  • Long Captions: Performance degrades as caption length increases. Long captions require capturing more details and complex relationships between objects, which is challenging for current models.
  • Implication: For applications requiring detailed descriptions, models might need specialized training on longer text or different architectures (e.g., larger context windows).

Summary of Results

Best Performing Model

BLIP consistently achieved the highest CIDEr scores across all experiments, demonstrating superior robustness and generalization.

Efficiency Winner

ViT-GPT2 offered the fastest inference times, making it suitable for real-time applications where latency is critical, despite slightly lower accuracy.
